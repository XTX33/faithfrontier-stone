This case concerns a Law Division civil action brought by Plaintiff Devon Tyler Barber against
Defendants John W. Tumelty, Esq. and the Law Office of John W. Tumelty. The action arises from
Defendants’ alleged failure to perform agreed legal services after accepting a retainer,
including alleged abandonment, non-communication, and failure to pursue available pretrial
remedies.

The Court granted reassignment to **Track 3**, recognizing the case’s complexity and the
presence of multiple independent tort, contract, and statutory claims.

All pleadings, court orders, certifications, and correspondence are cataloged in the docket list
and supporting filings directory for transparency and record integrity.

## Status Overview

- **October 3, 2025** – Verified Complaint and Civil Case Information Statement filed, initiating
  the Law Division action and setting forth the alleged retainer agreement, pretrial conduct, and
  claimed damages.

- **October 7, 2025** – Track Assignment Notice issued placing the matter on Team 102. Plaintiff
  filed related procedural submissions, including proposed orders and subpoenas, on the same
  date.

- **October 16–28, 2025** – Plaintiff submitted supplemental filings and an amended complaint to
  clarify factual allegations and legal theories.

- **November 7, 2025** – The Court entered an **Order Granting Track Change**, assigning the
  matter to Track 3 pursuant to Rule 4:5A based on complexity and scope.

- **November 24–25, 2025** – Defendants filed a motion to dismiss in lieu of an answer under
  Rule 4:6-2(e). Plaintiff thereafter filed a Second Amended Complaint and opposition papers.
  The motion remains pending.
